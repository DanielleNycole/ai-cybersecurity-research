# How Hackers Are Using AI to Scale Phishing Attacks

Everyone knows phishing emails are a major threat.
Common flags we tell users to look out for are grammar issues, or bad spelling.
AI is helpiong them change that and now they're getting good at it, and tricking users. 

## What's Happening:
AI is making the process easier than ever to generate highly convincing phishing messages at a large scale. 

Instead of sending generic emails with obvious red flags, attackers can use AI to: 
- Mimic tone and writing style
- Personalize messages based on public data
- Generate thousands of variations in seconds
This lowers the barrier to entry and increases success rate

## How attackers are using it:
- Scraping Linkedin or social media for personal details
- Generating realistic emails that look like coworkers  or managers
- Creating fake longin pages with near-perfect accuracy

All of this helps them automate their processes to move faster, avoid detection, and increase believability and trust. 

IBM tested Humans vs AI in creating phishing attacks. The AI took 5 prompts in 5 minutes to create a phishing email, and compared it to a phishing email that was human produced that took 16 hours. 
The human generated email was slightly more effective. Which is great news right? Not quite, the LLM only took 5 minutes and can improve significantly compared to a human not to mention, the LLM can produce 192 emails to every 1 email the human can produce. 

[IBM Humans VS AI Who's better at Phishing? ](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7XhySwUn9eA)

Most LLMs that are commonly used to the general public will not assist with this, however, there are alternative LLMS on the dark web that will generate without restrictions. 

## How we can defend against this:
Defenses need to evolve alongside the threats
- Email filtering with behavioral analysis 
- Multi-factor authentication
- Zero trust principles
- User education focused on context, not just grammar or spelling errors
- Continue to research and educate as techniques develop and grow
